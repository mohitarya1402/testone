FAQ – ANSWERS TO ALL THINGS ELECTRIC

CHARGING

Can a PHEV run on gas only?

The Mazda CX-90 PHEV doesn’t ever run purely on the gas engine. While the gas engine will, at times, serve as the CX-90 PHEV’s primary power source, it also acts as a generator to ensure the EV battery remains at a minimum state of charge to provide a seamless hybrid drive experience.

How can I charge my PHEV at home?

Thanks to its versatile charging port, charging a Mazda CX-90 PHEVis easy. Using the portable Level 1 charging cable that comes with your CX-90 PHEV, simply plug your vehicle into a regular wall socket to draw electricity directly from the grid. If you are looking to get the job done faster, you can install a universal Level 2 home charger to deliver a faster supply of power.

How often do I need to charge my PHEV?

For the optimal driving experience, charge every night. Plug-in and gradually top up the battery when not in use. For long drives or those weekends away when your battery runs low, your gas engine is there to provide backup power for your PHEV, taking you further until you are able to recharge again.

What kind of charging port is on the CX-90 PHEV?

The J1772 port on the CX-90 PHEV is configured to work with Level 1 and Level 2 charging cables.

Does the CX-90 PHEV come with a charging cable?

Yes, the CX-90 PHEV comes standard with a portable Level 1 charging cable to plug into common 120-volt outlets.

Can the Mazda CX-90 PHEV be charged in the rain?

Yes. Customers are not exposed to risk of electric shock when charging in the rain. However, we recommend you do not charge the high voltage battery outside if there is heavy rain, strong winds or lightning.

Remove water with a clean cloth if the charge connector or charge plug is wet. Avoid touching the charge port or charge cable with wet hands.

The charging port is designed so that it isn’t a problem if small amounts of water or dust get in the port when charging the car. Additionally, there is a cap that covers the charging port and a partitioning seal that helps keep the port dry and clean.

How do I find public charging stations near me?

You can visit Chargepoint https://na.chargepoint.com/charge_point to find public charging stations near you. Owners can also use the MyMazda App to search for and navigate to public charging stations near their home, workplace or other destinations. In addition, owners can also use the ChargePoint app to explore charging stations, view user-generated reviews, check current pricing and pay for their charging sessions.

Can I plug my Mazda CX-90 PHEV vehicle in at any public charging station?

The CX-90 PHEV is compatible with Level 2 charging solutions found at many public charging stations, but not Level 3 (DC Fast) solutions. PHEV cannot charge at Tesla Supercharging stations and is not compatible with the CHAdeMO DC Connector.
RANGE

Is there a way to optimize the range?

Yes. Like fuel efficiency with gas engines, your speed and the use of electrical features within the car that rely on battery power, such as air conditioning or heated seats, may also affect the vehicle’s range. Reducing the energy consumption of the vehicle will increase the range. To maximize driving range, consider these steps:

  • Pre-heating or pre-cooling your vehicle’s cabin via the MyMazda App while charging.1
  • Parking in a garage to minimize the effects from extreme weather.
  • Drive efficiently by avoiding heavy acceleration and high speeds, which can drain the battery faster.
  • Check your tire pressure often; underinflated tires can negatively impact range.

How does temperature affect Mazda electrified vehicles’ driving range?

Lithium-ion batteries prefer an ideal temperature of 77-95° F (25-35° C). If the battery is too cold, range, charging times and performance can decline. Range is less affected by hot weather, but the battery can degrade more quickly and charge more slowly.

What if I need to drive a longer distance and have range anxiety?

Since the CX-90 PHEV is a gasoline/electric hybrid vehicle, the gasoline engine is there to back up the electric motor when battery charge becomes low. You can fill your PHEV up with gas at any available gas station.
BATTERY

What can I do if I run out of battery charge on the road?

The CX-90 PHEV switches over to the gas engine for power when battery charge drops below a certain level. While driving, CX-90 PHEV can also employ “Charge Mode” that will use the engine to charge the battery up to a preset level of charge.

Do electrified cars give off emissions?

When the CX-90 PHEV is operated in electric-only mode, there are no emissions from the vehicle tailpipe. During longer driving distances, when the engine turns on, the PHEV performs similar to a traditional hybrid vehicle, consuming less fuel and producing fewer emissions than similar conventional vehicles.

While the CX-90 PHEV can operate on 100% electric power, you cannot operate the vehicle without gasoline in the tank.

What kind of battery is equipped with the Mazda CX-90 PHEV?

CX-90 PHEV is equipped with a 17.8 kWh lithium-ion battery.

Is the battery covered by a warranty?

Yes, the CX-90 PHEV lithium-ion batteries come with a High Voltage Battery Limited Warranty and a High Voltage Battery Capacity Limited Warranty. Both warranties are for 96 months or 100,000 miles, whichever comes first. In TZEV states, extended emission warranty coverage of the High Voltage Battery may apply. See your Mazda Dealer for Limited Warranty details

Can car batteries be recycled?

The batteries used in plug-in hybrids can be recycled. The batteries are lithium-ion type—just like those in laptops and mobile phones
